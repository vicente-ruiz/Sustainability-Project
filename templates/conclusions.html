Conclusions

Economic Sustainability:

  • Findings: AI could contribute up to $15.7 trillion to the global economy in 2030, but it could also widen gaps among countries, companies, and workers.
  • To-Dos: Policies should be implemented to ensure that the economic benefits of AI are distributed equitably. Education and training programs could be developed to prepare the workforce for the AI economy.

Social Sustainability:

  • Findings: AI has the potential to help tackle some of the worldâ€™s most challenging social problems, but it may also have a disruptive effect on the economy and society.
  • To-Dos: Social policies and regulations should be put in place to manage the societal impact of AI. Efforts should be made to use AI for social good.

Environmental Sustainability:

  • Findings: The use of AI comes with environmental costs, but it can also contribute to solving environmental problems.
  • To-Dos: More research should be done to reduce the environmental impact of AI. AI should be leveraged to assist research and foster effective environmental governance.
